Busia Health Care Services | HMS Platform Overview

Introduction This document provides a complete overview of the Busia Health Care
Services Hospital Management System (HMS) — a modern, web-based platform
designed to streamline clinical and administrative operations across the
facility.

It covers how the platform is accessed, who uses it, and what each user can do.
It is intended as a reference guide ahead of full deployment.

Quick Summary The HMS is a web application — nothing to install. Every staff
member and patient accesses it from any browser. Staff accounts are created and
controlled by the Admin. Patients can self-register or be registered at the
front desk.

1.  How the Platform is Delivered The HMS is a web-based application. There is
    no software to install from an app store — every member of staff and every
    patient accesses the same website from their phone, tablet, or computer.

Installing on a Personal Device (PWA) Because the platform is built as a
Progressive Web App (PWA), it can be installed on any device in seconds and
behaves exactly like a native app — full screen, home screen icon, no browser
bar.

On Android (Chrome):

1.  Open Chrome and visit the HMS website
2.  Tap the menu (three dots, top right)
3.  Tap "Add to Home Screen" or "Install App"
4.  The app icon appears on the home screen

On iPhone / iPad (Safari): 5. Open Safari and visit the HMS website 6. Tap the
Share button (box with arrow, bottom centre) 7. Tap "Add to Home Screen" 8. The
app icon appears on the home screen

2.  Who Uses the Platform The platform has six distinct roles. Each role sees
    only what is relevant to their work — no more, no less.

| Role                 | Who                 | What They Can Do                                                               |
| :------------------- | :------------------ | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Admin / Receptionist | Front desk staff    | Register patients, book appointments, manage records, create staff accounts    |
| Doctor               | Physicians          | View appointments, write medical records, issue prescriptions, order lab tests |
| Nurse                | Nursing staff       | View patient info, appointments, and medical records (read access)             |
| Pharmacist           | Pharmacy staff      | View and manage prescriptions and dispensing                                   |
| Lab Technician       | Laboratory staff    | Manage lab orders and enter lab results                                        |
| Patient              | Registered patients | View own appointments, medical records, lab results, and profile               |

3.  How Staff Get Access — The Invite System Staff accounts are never created by
    the staff member themselves. The Admin controls who has access to the
    system, ensuring no unauthorised person can create an account.

Step-by-Step: Onboarding a New Staff Member

Step 1 — Admin adds the staff member The Admin logs into the HMS, navigates to
Staff Management, and fills in the staff member's full name, email address, and
role.

Step 2 — System sends an invite email The staff member automatically receives a
secure invite link by email. The email reads:

Sample Invite Email "You have been invited to join the Busia Health Care
Services HMS. Click the link below to set up your password. This link expires
in 48 hours."

Step 3 — Staff member sets their own password The staff member clicks the link,
chooses a personal password, and their account is activated. The link works only
once and expires after 48 hours.

Step 4 — Staff member logs in They visit the HMS website, enter their email and
password, and land on their personalised dashboard.

Why This Approach • Passwords are never sent over email — only a one-time link
is sent • Links expire after 48 hours — intercepted emails become useless • The
Admin has full control over who is on the system at all times • Any staff member
can be deactivated instantly if they leave the clinic

4.  How Patients Get Access Patients have two ways to register with the system.

Option A — Self-Registration (Web or Phone) A patient visits the HMS website and
clicks "Create an account" on the login page. They fill in personal details
(name, date of birth, gender, blood type, phone, address) along with their email
and password. The account is created immediately.

Option B — Registered by the Receptionist The receptionist registers the patient
on their behalf at the front desk — whether a walk-in, phone call, or referral.
The receptionist can optionally create a login account for the patient at the
same time.

What Patients See When They Log In • My Profile — personal details (phone number
and address are editable) • My Appointments — upcoming and past appointments •
My Medical Records — diagnoses and treatment notes from consultations

Privacy Guarantee Patients cannot see any other patient's information. They see
only their own.

5.  Daily Workflows The Receptionist's Dashboard The receptionist is the primary
    day-to-day user of the system. Their dashboard is designed as a command
    centre — everything needed is one click away.

• Patient quick-search bar — type a name, phone number, or email • Today's
schedule — all appointments listed with patient names and doctors • Quick action
buttons — Register patient and Book appointment

Clicking on a patient opens their full profile with three tabs: • Overview —
demographics, next appointment, recent records • Appointments — full history
(upcoming and past) • Medical Records — all consultation notes and diagnoses

The Doctor's Workflow When a doctor logs in they see the appointments schedule
with clinical tools unlocked: • Create medical records — write consultation
notes, diagnosis, and treatment plan • Link records to appointments — every
record can be tied to the specific appointment • View full patient history — all
past records for any patient they are treating

6.  Security

Current Security Features • All passwords are encrypted before being stored —
even administrators cannot see them • All communication is encrypted (HTTPS) —
data is secure in transit • Each staff member has a personal login — actions are
traceable to the individual • Patient data is role-restricted — a pharmacist
cannot see medical records • Staff accounts can be deactivated instantly by the
Admin

Planned: Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) In a future update, staff will be
able to enable two-factor authentication. After entering their password, a
one-time code is sent to their phone, adding a second layer of protection —
particularly valuable for doctors and administrators.

Ready to Go The system is already designed to support MFA without any
restructuring. It will be activated in an upcoming release.
